Ford GoBike System Data¶

by Chisom Maduka¶

Introduction¶

Ford GoBike is a regional public bicycle sharing system covering the greater part of San Francisco Bay area, and represents the first regional and large-scale bicycle sharing system deployed in California and on the West Coast of the United States.¶

In this project, data visulization will be performed by using univariate, bivariate, and multivariate plots to explore and gather more information about individual Ford GoBike user behaviours and preferences by studying trends, changes and differences in the month of February 2019.¶

What is the structure of your dataset?¶

The FordGoBike datasets provide a valuable collection of data used to draw data findings and conclusion. This notebook uses data collected in February 2019, having about 183412 bike ride data entries with a total of 16 columns namely; ('duration_sec', 'start_time', 'end_time', 'start_station_id','start_station_name', 'start_station_latitude', 'start_station_longitude', 'end_station_id', 'end_station_name', 'end_station_latitude', 'end_station_longitude', 'bike_id', 'user_type', 'member_birth_year', 'member_gender', 'bike_share_for_all_trip')

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

I intend to focus on the Ride ID, Trip Duration, Start Time and Date,End Time and Date,Distance of each Trip and User Type, which I believe will help me understand different patterns that will answer the following questions:

  • At what time of the week recorded the most frequent trips?
  • What is the proportion of subscriber and customer bike riders, and who frequents the most?
  • What is the relationship between the trip duration and ages for subscriber and customer users?
  • Which user is likely to be the potential audience to target more on?

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

The features in the dataset that I think will ehance my investigation would be the user types, start times, end times as well as any related time-based information.

Conclusions¶

Key Findings in Univariate Exploration¶

  1. The distribution of the trip duration was highly skewed to the right (positive skewness), and the trip duration was mostly around 3 to 15 minutes
  2. The peak of trip visits was usually during rush hour, typically in the morning (between 8 and 9am) and evening (between 4 to 6pm)
  3. Majority of the users were the subscribers, who happen to be registered members
  4. Majority of the travels made was on weekdays while a few travels was made on weekends
  5. Young adults ages 18-40 years travelled more frequently, followed by the older adults (21%) and the least being seniors above 70 years, with little or no significant presence.
  6. San Fransisco Caltrain Station 2 (Townsend St at 4th St) was mostly visited.

Key Findings in Bivariate Exploration¶

  1. The older the age, the less rides and shorter distance observed
  2. Subscribers had the highest population across all age brackets
  3. Customer users have longer trip duration compared with subscribers
  4. Users were be more active on weekdays than weekends.
  5. The ratio of subscribers and customers travelling on each day is highly significant across all time of the day, with over 80% of subscribers travelling every hour.

Key Findings in Multivariate Exploration¶

  1. Many users ride bikes mainly on weekdays from Monday all through to Friday, around 7-9am and 4-6pm.
  2. The younger ones happened to travel longer, generating more revenue than the older ones. Hence they can be considered as the target audience.
  3. Users that frequent the most are ages 18 to 45 years, and this falls within the young_adults range and are mostly young subscribers.
  4. Weekdays took the lead in terms of revenue generation
  5. Although a larger distance was covered on weekends for all genders (male, female and others), this does not prove the total number of bikers and hence, no reasonable conclusion can be drawn.
